Sunflower

The sunflower (Helianthus annuus) is a large, annual plant native to the Americas. It is known for its bright yellow petals and large, round flower head that can reach up to 30 centimeters in diameter. The plant can grow several meters tall and is famous for its ability to track the movement of the sun across the sky, a phenomenon known as heliotropism. Sunflowers are cultivated for their edible seeds, which are a source of oil and snacks, and they are also grown for their vibrant appearance, making them popular in gardens and floral arrangements.

The white water lily (Nymphaea alba) is an aquatic perennial plant known for its large, fragrant white flowers that float on the surface of ponds and slow-moving water bodies.

The Moth Orchid (Phalaenopsis) is a popular houseplant known for its long-lasting, colorful flowers that resemble moths in flight.

The Sacred Lotus (Nelumbo nucifera) is an aquatic plant revered for its large, fragrant pink or white flowers and its cultural significance in various religions.
